Welcome to the Legacy Context Tracker

This tool helps you visually map and compare experiences, emotions, or decisions across six key categories. Whether tracking personal growth, team dynamics, or creative collaboration, this system allows multiple users to mark and analyze their perspectives in a shared space.

How It Works

Placement Matters: Dots placed closer to the center represent internal experiences (thoughts, feelings, personal growth). Dots placed further out represent external influences (actions, interactions, and outward expression). Color Codes & Meaning: Each color represents a core principle, helping define the nature of the experience. Closer to one principle = a stronger expression of that trait. Equally positioned between principles = a balanced alignment between them. Subjective, Not Absolute: This system reflects personal perception, not fixed values. Every placement is an estimate, meant to track and compare perspectives over time.

Understanding Your Map

The Emotional Mapping System

How Emotions Are Placed on the Map

The Legacy Context Tracker visualizes and compares emotional states and experiences across six core principles. Placement on the map affects meaning based on proximity to the center, the edges, or between principles.

Core Emotional Mapping by Principle

Each principle represents a different aspect of human experience. Emotional states tied to these principles evolve based on their internal vs. external focus.

Power & Purpose (Red)

- Internal: Confidence, ambition, self-determination, willpower. - External: Leadership, control over others, assertiveness.Wisdom & Adaptation (Blue) - Internal: Reflection, deep thought, intellectual curiosity. - External: Learning from others, adaptability in dynamic situations. - Balanced: Someone who embraces new ideas but applies structured wisdom.

Connection & Impact (Purple)

- Internal: Personal love, deep emotional bonds, connection to others. - External: Influence on groups, social change, teamwork. - Balanced: Someone fostering relationships while advocating for collective growth.

Action & Method (Orange)

- Internal: Focus, strategy, discipline, personal effort. - External: Taking decisive action, managing projects, executing plans. - Balanced: A strategic planner balancing thoughtfulness with execution.

Growth & Balance (Green)

- Internal: Personal development, healing, self-acceptance. - External: Mentoring others, creating long-term stability. - Balanced: Someone improving themselves while fostering stability for others.

Truth & Identity (Yellow)

- Internal: Self-awareness, honesty with oneself, deep personal reflection. - External: Expressing truth outwardly, challenging norms, defining a personal brand. - Balanced: Someone true to themselves while effectively communicating their values.

How Placement Affects Meaning

Center (Internal Focus) - Represents personal thoughts, emotions, and inner struggles. - Example: A dot in the center near Truth & Identity may indicate self-reflection or identity crisis.

Outer Rings (External Focus)

- Reflects how emotions influence actions, decisions, and interactions. - Example: A dot near Power & Purpose at the edge suggests strong leadership or control.

Between Two Areas (Blended Influence)

- Indicates a balance of those traits. - Example: A dot between Connection & Growth suggests improving relationships while evolving personally.

Subjectivity and Context

- This system is not absolute; every placement is based on personal perception. - Dots serve as guides, tracking emotional and mental states over time. - Repeated patterns provide insight into personal growth, recurring struggles, or evolving priorities.

Next Steps for Users

1. Reflect on the emotion or experience you want to track. 2. Choose a position on the map based on how internal or external the feeling is. 3. Compare your placements with others to identify patterns and misunderstandings. 4. Adjust over time to see how your perspectives evolve. This tool is a living reflection of thoughts and emotions, helping make sense of personal experiences and the perspectives of others.
